ChameleonVoice: A Contextual Prosody Perception Network for Emotional Voice Conversion

Tianhua Qi1,2, Cheng Lu1,2, Yuan Zong1,2, Wenming Zheng1,2, Björn W. Schuller3,4
1 School of Biological Science and Medical Engineering, Southeast University, China
2 Key Laboratory of Child Development and Learning Science (Southeast University), Ministry of Education, China
3 Chair of Health Informatics (CHI), Technical University of Munich, Germany

1. Abstract

Emotional voice conversion (EVC) has progressed rapidly, yet generating speech with natural prosody remains a core unsolved problem. The primary reason is that existing approaches rely heavily on static emotion categories, pseudo-labeled prosodic attributes, or reference-driven transfer mechanisms, which fail to model emotional prosody in a contextually aligned manner. Consequently, current systems often exhibit over-templated rhythmic patterns, misplaced emphasis, and limited perceptual naturalness. To address these challenges, we propose ChameleonVoice, a contextual prosody perception network that models emotional prosody as a temporally evolving process jointly shaped by linguistic structure and continuous affective state. The framework incorporates a contextual prosody perception mechanism that learns how rhythm, stress, and intonation unfold across the utterance based on semantic cues, and integrates valence–arousal emotion descriptors to capture intra-category variability and support fine-grained prosody modulation without requiring additional attributes or reference utterances. This design enables ChameleonVoice to adaptively reshape prosodic contours while preserving intelligibility and speaker identity. Comprehensive experiments across multiple emotions, speakers, and languages demonstrate that ChameleonVoice substantially improves prosodic naturalness, emotional expressiveness, and contextual coherence over state-of-the-art EVC systems.
